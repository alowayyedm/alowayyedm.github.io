About Me

I am a PhD candidate in the Interactive Intelligence Group at TU Delft, supervised by Dr. Willem-Paul Brinkman and Dr. Myrthe Tielman. I earned my MSc in Computer Science from TU Delft in 2020, and my BSc in Software Engineering (cum laude) from King Saud University (KSU) in 2016.

My research focuses on Human-Computer Interaction, Behavior Change Technologies, Conversational Agents, and Educational Systems. I design interactive training tools that simulate social interactions to support social skill development, such as virtual agents for child helpline counselor training.

I have co-supervised five MSc theses students and over a dozen BSc students. I am also a member of the PROTECt ME Consortium and TU Delft’s Ethics Review Team.
